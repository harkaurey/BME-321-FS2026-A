## Efficacy of a Novel Antihypertensive Drug in treating Stage 1 Hypertension

A randomized controlled trial is conducted to evaluate the effectiveness of a new antihypertensive drug (AH-27) compared to standard care.

Patients diagnosed with stage 1 hypertension are randomized to receive either the new drug or standard therapy for 8 weeks. Blood pressure is measured at baseline and at the end of the study.

***The primary endpoint is the reduction in systolic blood pressure (SBP).***

### Variables

-   *Outcome*:

    reduction_sbp: change in systolic blood pressure (mmHg) from baseline to 8 weeks

-   *Continuous predictors*:

    -   age: patient age (years)
    -   baseline_sbp: Baseline systolic blood pressure (mmHg)

-   *Categorical predictors:*

    -   treatment_group: randomized treatment ("standard_care", "AH27")
    -   sex: biological sex ("female", "male")

### Hypotheses:

-   The new drug (AH-27) leads to a greater reduction in SBP
-   Higher baseline SBP is associated with a larger reduction
-   Older patients will have reduced responsiveness
-   Small sex difference
